Sundai Edo
One can clearly see similarities between Sundai Edo and Hokusai's arguably most famous artwork, The Wave of Kangawa. The images of Mount Fuji towering in the background, the pastel-like colours of nature as well as the style of the Japanese people and their clothing. While the thirty six paintings are Houkusai's most notable pieces on Mount Fuji, he released a book later on titled One hundred views of Mount Fuji.
Sundai Edo itself portrays Japanese farmers at work in the Edo period. Meanwhile, Mount Fuji looms far in the distance and examples of traditional Japanese architecture is visible in the foreground. With the trees a dark shade of green and the ground being verdant and grassy, it's likely a depiction of Japan's luscious summer.
